The Minor

The interdisciplinary minor in computational social science (CSS) at UC San Diego combines formal causal models from the social sciences with statistics, programming, and large-scale data analysis. Students enrolled in the minor will take three courses in computational social science and at least four upper-division electives, comprising twenty-eight total units of which at least twenty (five courses) must be upper division. Upper-division courses may be selected from among the following courses. At least two courses must be taken from departments that are not the department of the student’s major. Students may petition to receive credit for courses not listed below. Advising for the minor is housed in the Department of Psychology.

Students are advised to strongly consider MATH 10A-B-C or MATH 20A-B-C and MATH 18 as preparation for upper-division electives.
